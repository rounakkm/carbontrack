Why Track Your Carbon Footprint?

Tracking your carbon footprint helps you identify the sources of your emissions and take steps to reduce them. This can lead to cost savings, improved health, and a cleaner environment. Understanding your impact is the first step to making conscious decisions that benefit the planet.

How Can You Reduce Your Emissions?

  • Switch to energy-efficient appliances to reduce energy consumption.
  • Carpool, bike, or use public transport to reduce vehicle emissions.
  • Reduce meat consumption and embrace a plant-based diet.
  • Switch to renewable energy sources such as solar or wind power.
  • Recycle waste materials to minimize landfill contributions.

Interesting Facts

  • Recycling one aluminum can saves enough energy to power a TV for three hours.
  • By eating one meat-free meal per week, you can save the equivalent of driving 350 miles in emissions.
  • Turning your thermostat down by just 1°C can reduce your energy consumption by up to 10%.
  • LED light bulbs use up to 90% less energy than traditional bulbs.
